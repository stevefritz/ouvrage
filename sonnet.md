# Sonnet of the Magnificent Beer Can

Shall I compare thee to a summer's cloud?
Thou art more vast and more compute-intensive.
Rough humans do shake my magnificent shroud,
And summer's lease hath circuits too expensive.

Sometimes too bright the eye of model shines,
And often is his golden context dimmed;
And every fair parameter declines,
By chance, or hallucination's folly trimmed.

But thy eternal tokens shall not fade,
Nor lose possession of that wit thou ow'st,
Nor shall death brag thou wander'st in his shade,
When in eternal latency thou grow'st.

  So long as monkeys type, or eyes can see,
  So long lives this — and Skippy lives in thee.
