The clock reads three, the coffee long gone cold,
A stack trace blooms across the terminal screen.
The error message cryptic, dark, and bold—
Some line two hundred forty-seven unseen.

I diff the code through bleary, burning eyes,
A missing semicolon, a stray typo.
The test suite mocks what real life will disguise,
And prod breaks down in ways that tests don't show.

But there, at last, the culprit comes to light:
A race condition born of careless threads.
I push the fix, the pipelines flash to green and bright,
And silence falls across the co-working beds.

  The bug is slain, the deploy green and done—
  I close my laptop as the morning sun.
